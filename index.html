CSE 482 Big Data Analysis (Spring 2026)

This course is intended for senior undergraduate students who are interested in gaining hands-on experience applying computational techniques to solve big data analysis problems. Examples of such problems include: How to detect trending topics in social media data? How to predict customers who would churn to a competitor? How to design a large-scale recommender system? Students will learn the basic techniques and programming tools that can be used to provide answers to such questions.

Preference Books

There is no main textbook for the class. Lecture notes will be posted online and are based on materials from the following books:

  • Introduction to Data Mining (Pang-Ning Tan, Michael Steinbach, and Vipin Kumar), Addison Wesley, 2006
  • Mining of Massive Datasets (Anand Rajaraman, Jeff Ullman), Cambridge University Press, 2011
  • Hadoop: The Definitive Guide (Tom White), O'Reilly Media, Fourth Edition, 2015

Course Policy
